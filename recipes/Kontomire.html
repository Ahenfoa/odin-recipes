KONTOMIRE STEW RECIPE

KONTOMIRE(PALAVA SAUCE)

Description

Kontomire stew is a stew made from cocoyam leaves (known in the Akan language as "kontomire"), commonly prepared in the home and very popular in Ghanaian cuisine. In Ghana, kontomire stew is served with variety of dishes, including steamed rice, cooked yam and plantain.

Ingredients

  • Kontomire leaves
  • 5-8 tomatoes
  • a hand full of turkey berries
  • 4 large onions
  • 2-3 green bell peppers
  • red palm oil
  • a handful of green chilies
  • melon powder
  • eggs
  • shrimp powder
  • spring onion
  • beef
  • wele
  • momoni and kako
  • smoked salmon
  • seasoning cube
  • seasoning powder
  1. Wash kontomire leaves with a generous amount of salt to prevent itching of hands when cutting. Cut kontomire into desired stripes. Put the cut kotomire into a saucepan with little water and place on high heat. Cover and bring to a boil. Turn the kontomire after about five minutes of cooking to make sure everything is cooked through. Turn off fire and set aside.
  2. Blend three onions, tomatoes, pepper and turkey berries together, set aside. Remove the flesh of the salmon, break into two and remove the bones, break into desired sizes, wash beef and wele and set aside. Slice the remaining onion, green bell peppers and spring onions.
  3. Pour palm oil into a medium saucepan, add the sliced onions, kako and momoni. Fry till onions are softened. Add the blended tomatoes, onions, turkey berries and pepper. Bring to a simmer on medium heat. Add the salmon,wele, seasoning cube and seasoning powder and grounded shrimp if using. Cover and let simmer for 7 to 10 minutes till sauce is bare thickened.
  4. Add in the melon powder and eggs, DO NOT STIR, cover and reduce heat to low. Let it simmer 8 to 10 minutes or till the melon and egg sets. Gently stir in the steamed kontomire. Add the remaining sliced onions, let simmer for about five minutes till onions are softened. Add sliced green bell peppers and spring onions. Serve with yam, rice, cocoyam, cassava, plantains or sweet potatoes.
